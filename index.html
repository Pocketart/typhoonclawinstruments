Typhoon Claw Instruments

Our team has designed and built 5 innovative digital instruments to provide a unique music learning experience by allowing students to play instruments in a different way. These instruments will integrate with the flow.ai music notation system in a modern approach to music education.

Dance Pad Drums

BeatsDrums

This is a virtual drum pad set created by remapping the inputs of a Wii Dance Pad so that each tile represents a different sound that can be generated using a standard drummer's set. The location of each sound is relative to the location of each instruement on a real drum set, allowing students to get familiar with where each instrument is located in a more interesting way.

In order to build this we will first open up the main controller box of the dance pad. Then we will connect the output ports of each sensor to an arudino controller board. We can then map the inputs from the dance pad into a computer via a usb cable and use it alonside with the flat.io API.

DJ Board

TunesMixing

The DJ Board features a drum pad that make note by pressing it. The slider sensor at the left can change the sound but controlling different value such as volume, key, range and note. The two small button below the drum button is able to change note to sharp and flat, and the switch right beside them is the on and off switch.

Students will play a beat on the drum pad which will play the note set by the sliders. The volume controls the volume of the system, while the key, range, and note will determine the exact note to play. Music played with the DJ Board will then be recorded for playback.

Iron Man Gloves

Gloves

The Iron Man Gloves are designed to be used in a similar way to playing percussion instruments. The color sensors attached to the gloves will return different RGB values when the user’s hands touch or hit objects of different colors. Each RGB value corresponds to a musical note, thus when a series of objects are hit, the array of notes could be composed into a piece of music.

Blue:Blue: Nano Arduino V3.0
Yellow: Color sensor
Red: Wires that connect the arduino and the sensor

Musical Car

MusicalCar

The Musical Car is an instrumental project built upon the car used in last year’s PAS Annual Musical Grease. The design includes a steering wheel, two pedals (only one is functional), a gear and the car itself (the car is movable but only when someone pushes from behind). By steering the wheel, children are able to change the pitch, or frequency, of the sound. The accelerator pedal (functional one) controls the volume. If no forces is exerted, the volume will be zero and thus no sound will be produced. The gear enables children to change the mode of the sound. For example, sounds of an piano, a guitar, and a violin. When playing this car instrument, another student can push from the back. Children can not only enjoy the fun of playing music but also find them having a exciting ride.

All of the three key components will use potentiometers, which will alter the resistance depending on the angle rotated at the joint. The steering wheel should mainly consist of wood and rubber that simulate the actual texture of a functioning steering wheel; the gear shift and the accelerator pedal should mainly be made of wood. Inside the gear shift there should be different open circuits, which will be closed when the gear shift lever is moved to correct positions. The design will incorporate students’ laptops and connect all three components with Arduino, using it as a signal transmitter that will tell the computer which note should be played.

Sound of Music

LightPillar

The structure is made from PVC pipes. It will have one side covered with pipe (containing the circuits and arduino), and the three other sides hollow for the hand to freely move vertically. This instrument that constantly produces a sound and adjusts its pitch according to the height of your hand, which is detected by an ultrasonic sensor, similar to the video below:

Further, the green dots shown in the 3D model represents a LED light bulb, which will light up to the height that detects your hand(Starting from the bottom to the top). All the LED below the detected hand will light up, while the LEDs above the hand are turned off, forming a pillar light. Combining both the ultrasonic sensor and LED lights, whenever a hand is in the center of the structure, it will make sound at a specific pitch according to the height while displaying the hand’s height by lighting up LED lights up to the hand’s height.
